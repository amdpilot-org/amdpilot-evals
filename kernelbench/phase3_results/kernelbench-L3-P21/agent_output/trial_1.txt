• Now let me analyze the MBConv block structure. Looking at the reference:

  MBConv Block Structure:

  1. expand_conv (if expand_ratio != 1): 1x1 Conv + BN + ReLU6
    • Input: (batch, in_channels, H, W) → Output: (batch, hidden_dim, H, W)
    • hidden_dim = in_channels * expand_ratio = 112 * 6 = 672
  2. depthwise_conv: KxK Depthwise Conv + BN + ReLU6
    • Input: (batch, hidden_dim, H, W) → Output: (batch, hidden_dim, H/stride, W
      ide)
    • kernel_size=5, stride=2, padding=2, groups=hidden_dim
  3. project_conv: 1x1 Conv + BN (no activation)
    • Input: (batch, hidden_dim, H/stride, W/stride) → Output: (batch, out_chann
      H/stride, W/stride)
  4. Residual connection (if stride==1 and in_channels==out_channels): Not used
     e since stride=2

  Key observations for optimization:

  • stride=2 means output is 112x112 (half of 224x224)
  • No residual connection (stride != 1)
  • Three conv operations that could potentially be fused
  • BatchNorm operations can be folded into conv weights for inference
  • ReLU6 is a simple clamp: min(max(x, 0), 6)
